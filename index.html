Keyword Filtering in ATS Systems

A quick comparison of how three types of Applicant Tracking Systems filter resumes

Boolean-Based Filtering

Uses logic operators (AND, OR, NOT) to match keywords in resumes.

Example:
Query: ("JavaScript" AND "React") OR ("Node.js") NOT ("Intern")
Only resumes that mention JavaScript and React, or Node.js—but not Intern—will be included.

AI/NLP-Based Filtering

Uses Natural Language Processing to understand context, semantics, and synonyms.

Example:
The system understands "Python developer" ≈ "experienced in scripting with Python".

Rule-Based Filtering

Applies predefined rules to include/exclude resumes.

Example:
Rule: Must include “SQL” AND “3+ years experience”
If not exact, resume may be excluded.
